Air pollution is often described in micrograms per cubic metre. That is technically
correct, but it is not always emotionally or politically legible.

The purpose of this blog is to translate particulate pollution into a metric that
people understand immediately: **years of life**.

## Why this blog exists

AQLI-style communication sits at the intersection of:

| Layer | Question |
|---|---|
| Atmospheric science | How polluted is the air? |
| Exposure science | Who breathes that air? |
| Epidemiology | What is the health penalty? |
| Policy analysis | What would change under a cleaner standard? |
| Communication | How do we explain it without distortion? |

## Editorial style

Each post should do three things:

1. explain the data
2. show the human meaning
3. state the caveats clearly

The aim is not to make pollution look dramatic. The aim is to make the cost of
pollution measurable.
